Icons

NexLed icons should feel precise, readable, and structurally aligned with the rest of the system.

System & Function

System & Function: How Icons Support the Brand

Icons are supporting elements, not visual protagonists. They should improve scanning, reinforce meaning, and stay visually compatible with the technical clarity of the NexLed system.

Status

Use simple, readable glyphs that communicate alerts or state without extra decoration.

Confirmation

Choose icons that remain legible at small sizes and do not depend on overly fine detail.

Product Context

Product-facing icons should feel geometric and compatible with the technical tone of the brand.

Channels

Social or communication icons should stay consistent with the same icon family and sizing logic.

Commerce

Use icons that explain intent quickly and avoid novelty or decorative alternatives.

Platform Fit

Approved channel icons can be used when they support context without overpowering the page.

Application Rules

Application Rules: Incorrect Uses to Avoid

Incorrect icon usage usually comes from inconsistency: the wrong family, the wrong color treatment, or formatting that fights the grid and hurts readability.

Incorrect

Do not introduce icon families outside the approved set.

Incorrect

Do not apply colors that pull icons outside the approved system context.

Incorrect

Do not crop, overscale, or reformat icons in ways that damage clarity.

Incorrect

Do not rotate icons or their carriers in ways that break alignment or readability.
