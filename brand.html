Tone of Voice

Brand Communication

Write as a technical partner, not as a campaign voice. The reader should understand what the solution is, where it fits, and why it matters without reading twice.

NexLed sounds informed, calm, and specific. The brand does not oversell, dramatize performance, or rely on generic innovation language.

Across proposals, catalogs, web pages, and social content, the voice should leave the same impression: this brand knows lighting, respects the buyer's context, and communicates with discipline.

How to Write

  • Start with the application or usage context

  • State the performance benefit in plain technical language

  • Use exact terms, numbers, and product facts where available

  • Keep claims grounded and easy to verify

  • End with the decision-relevant takeaway, not filler copy

Official Slogan

"Light for Every Need"

"Light for Every Need" works because it presents NexLed as a flexible, solution-led brand that can respond to different projects without sounding vague or generic.

Use it in introductions, overview materials, and brand-led communication. Do not use it as a substitute for product-level claims or technical descriptions.

Brand Architecture

NexLed is the product-facing lighting brand within the Tec IT group. Tec IT adds corporate credibility when needed, but NexLed should remain the name users remember when the conversation is about lighting solutions.

Tec IT

Corporate endorser

NexLed

Primary brand

Main Rule

Lead with NexLed on any asset centered on products, applications, or solutions. Add Tec IT only when ownership, legal context, or corporate trust needs to be made explicit.

This structure keeps the hierarchy readable, avoids brand competition inside the same layout, and protects NexLed as the specialist brand in the group.

Brand Application Rules

If both brands appear, NexLed remains primary and Tec IT stays secondary. The endorsement should support the message, not compete with it or repeat ownership unnecessarily.
