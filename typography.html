Why the Typeface Works

Urbanist complements the logo by keeping the system geometric, clean, and easy to scale across interfaces, documents, and technical communication.

Hierarchy should come from weight, size, spacing, and contrast, never from decorative formatting or inconsistent type choices.

Type Scale & Usage

  • •

    Use Urbanist across web, print, and presentation surfaces to preserve a single typographic voice.

  • •

    Create hierarchy through size, spacing, and weight, not through ornamental effects.

  • •

    Choose text color based on contrast and the approved brand palette.

Main headlines

Print: 24-34 pt. Web: 40-56 px.

Section titles

Print: 18-24 pt. Web: 28-40 px.

Subsections

Print: 16-20 pt. Web: 22-28 px.

Paragraph text

Print: 13-15 pt. Web: 16-18 px.

Captions & metadata

Print: 11-15 pt. Web: 12-14 px.

Typography Mistakes to Avoid

Incorrect

Mixing unrelated weights inside the same message breaks clarity and rhythm.

Incorrect

Ignoring the scale makes section hierarchy harder to scan and understand.

Incorrect

Using text colors outside the approved palette weakens consistency and readability.

Incorrect

Decorative formatting such as excessive styling or crossed text should not define hierarchy.

Incorrect

Using non-brand typefaces fragments the identity and reduces system cohesion.
